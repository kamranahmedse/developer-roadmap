# Ratio Metrics

Ratio metrics compare two numerical quantities by dividing one by the other. This results in a relative measure that can be used to understand the proportional relationship between the two values. Common examples include conversion rates, profit margins, or even simple metrics like students per teacher. The resulting ratio provides a standardized way to compare values across different groups or time periods, even when the underlying absolute values vary significantly.
